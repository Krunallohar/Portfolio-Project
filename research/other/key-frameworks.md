# Key Frameworks — Newsletter & Email Marketing for B2B SaaS

Synthesized from research across 10 experts. These are the repeating patterns and frameworks that separate high-performing B2B SaaS newsletters from average ones.

---

## Framework 1: Newsletter as Product (Emily Kramer)
Treat your newsletter as a standalone product, not a marketing channel.
- It needs a clear value proposition
- It needs a defined audience (ICP)
- It needs a consistent format readers can rely on
- Every issue must be useful to someone who has never heard of your company

**Application:** Before writing issue #1, define: Who is this for? What will they get every week? Why would they miss it if it stopped?

---

## Framework 2: The Reverse Engineer Method (Chenell Basilio)
Instead of guessing what works, study newsletters that have already grown and reverse-engineer their exact tactics.
- Who did they collaborate with?
- What growth channels drove their first 1,000 subscribers?
- When did they introduce paid tiers?
- What content formats got the most forwards/shares?

**Application:** Pick 3 newsletters in your niche that have 10x your subscribers. Map their growth timeline. Find the 2-3 moves that drove the biggest jumps.

---

## Framework 3: Live Text First (Jay Schwedelson)
As AI summarizes emails before humans read them, the structure of your email matters more than ever.
- Use live HTML text for all key messages — not images
- AI systems (Gmail Gemini, Apple Intelligence, Outlook Copilot) read live text, not image-embedded text
- Subject lines and preview text are now being processed by AI before reaching the subscriber

**Application:** Audit your last 5 emails. How much of your key message lives inside images vs. actual HTML text? Shift to live text for all critical content.

---

## Framework 4: POV-Driven Content (MKT1 / Dave Gerhardt)
The newsletters that grow all have a clear point of view — a belief that most people don't hold.
- Generic "tips and tricks" newsletters don't build loyal audiences
- A strong POV means readers know exactly what you stand for
- POV creates word-of-mouth: people share newsletters that say something they agree with but haven't heard said before

**Application:** Write your newsletter's POV in one sentence. If you can't, that's the first thing to fix.

---

## Framework 5: Email as Relationship, Not Broadcast (Val Geisler / Samar Owais)
The best B2B SaaS email programs are built on behavioral triggers and lifecycle logic — not batch-and-blast campaigns.
- Onboarding sequences that respond to what users do (or don't do)
- Re-engagement campaigns triggered by inactivity
- Upgrade emails timed to usage milestones
- Churn prevention emails triggered by drop in engagement

**Application:** Map your customer lifecycle. Identify the 3 moments where a well-timed email could change behavior. Build those before anything else.

---

## Framework 6: Audience Before Product (Nathan Barry)
Kit (ConvertKit) was built on the back of Nathan Barry's own newsletter audience. The lesson: build your audience before you need it.
- A newsletter list of 1,000 engaged subscribers is more valuable than 10,000 cold contacts
- Even a small list has early adopters, pilot clients, and brand advocates in it
- The newsletter IS the business development engine for B2B SaaS

**Application:** Start your newsletter before you think you're ready. The compounding effect of consistent publishing means starting 6 months earlier is worth more than any paid acquisition later.
