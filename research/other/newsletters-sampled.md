# Newsletters Sampled

A collection of real newsletters reviewed during this research project.

---

## 1. MKT1 Newsletter — Emily Kramer
- **URL:** https://mkt1.substack.com
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **What it does well:** Every issue is built around a single actionable framework for B2B SaaS marketers. No fluff, no product promotion. Consistent POV throughout.
- **Key insight:** Treats the newsletter as a standalone product, not a content distribution channel.

---

## 2. Exit Five Newsletter — Dave Gerhardt
- **URL:** https://exitfive.com/newsletter
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **What it does well:** Mixes community insights with tactical marketing advice. Subject lines are direct and specific. Feels like it's written by a practitioner, not a brand.
- **Key insight:** 40,000+ subscribers built by giving away real operator knowledge consistently.

---

## 3. Growth in Reverse — Chenell Basilio
- **URL:** https://growthinreverse.com
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **What it does well:** Reverse-engineers how top newsletters grew. Each issue profiles one newsletter operator and breaks down their exact growth tactics.
- **Key insight:** Unique angle — studying growth rather than just producing content builds a highly engaged niche audience.

---

## 4. Lenny's Newsletter — Lenny Rachitsky
- **URL:** https://www.lennysnewsletter.com
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **What it does well:** Deep, well-researched posts with original data and frameworks. Guest posts from top operators. Strong community around it (paid membership).
- **Key insight:** The largest paid B2B newsletter. Proof that going deep on one topic for one audience compounds over time.

---

## 5. Do This Not That — Jay Schwedelson
- **URL:** https://subjectline.com
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **What it does well:** Every tip is backed by real send data from billions of emails. Short, punchy, immediately actionable format.
- **Key insight:** Data-driven positioning makes every issue feel credible and trustworthy — not opinion, but evidence.
