## Post 1 — 1 hour ago (June 2025)

Framer just announced Agents, built right into their website builder. This is not just another agent, but a true peanut butter & jelly situation.

Most AI tools are fine for building a single page. But building a whole site is a bit maddening (at least to my pixel-perfect-seeking brain). Nothing matches. Every new page feels like starting from scratch. What you get is hard to edit.

What Framer announced is different:
→ Framer Agents work directly on the Framer canvas and can design pages, manage CMS content, write copy, build components, and add SEO. It's all one system.
→ Every change is visible, editable, and under your control, and Branching lets you test and review everything you build with Agents before it goes live.

Here's the PB&J / better-together part:
+ A CMS (like Framer) gives your site structure: repeatable pages, components, and layouts that follow the same rules, so you can scale your site properly.
+ Agents give you speed, and the ability to design and build from a prompt.
= Together, these benefits compound.

Bottom line: new AI tools and agents shouldn't mean you need to throw out the stuff that still works. Framer is a great example of that.

## Post 2 — 1 day ago (June 2025)

Intercom → Fin → Salesforce

Apparently, my taste in billboards does not correlate with acquisition interest.

Intercom/Fin announced its acquisition today, but on my recent SF billboard tour I was less than impressed with their Fin billboards. I couldn't tell what was going on. What is that orange blob?

Intercom used to run clean, plain-text ads as billboards. Those stood out for being boring, and got coverage for the simplicity. This creative lands in a weird middle: not as clear as the plain-text ads, not memorable enough to count as great creative.

The other problem: the name change. It was unclear for a while if Intercom was now Fin, if Fin was just the agent, and I didn't even know an official name change happened in May until I read about the acquisition today.

So what's the lesson? Building brand equity takes a very long time. If you change your name or launch a sub-brand, use your existing brand credibility to bridge the gap. Don't make people guess who you are.
