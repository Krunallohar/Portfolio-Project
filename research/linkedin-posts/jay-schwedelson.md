## Post 3 — 6 hours ago (June 2025)

AI has entered its judgmental phase.

It's judging your website. It's judging your LinkedIn posts. It's judging your emails. And now AI is deciding whether actual humans ever see any of it.

**GOOGLE AI IS CHANGING THE GAME**

New data from Ahrefs found that only 38% of pages cited in Google's AI Overviews are ranking in Google's top 10 search results. One year ago that number was 76%.

What's working right now: making content look current. Not just updating the page — showing it's current.
Examples:
- "Email Deliverability Trends: June 2026"
- "What's Working in LinkedIn Ads Right Now? June 2026"

**LINKEDIN IS BECOMING AN AI GOLDMINE**

Meltwater found that 75% of LinkedIn citations appearing in AI-generated answers come from individual profiles rather than company pages. Not the brand. The people.

The most-cited LinkedIn content overwhelmingly uses: bullet points, clear section headings, recently published content.

Your leadership team's LinkedIn posts may now be more important for AI visibility than your company page.

**AI IS NOW READING YOUR EMAILS FIRST**

Gmail, Apple Intelligence, and Outlook Copilot are all generating AI summaries of emails and email threads before your subscriber ever reads them.

This means live text matters more than ever. Live text = actual HTML text inside your email. Words embedded inside an image are NOT live text. AI systems may not interpret them the same way.

The safest move: make sure your most important message exists as actual text in the email itself — not just inside the creative.

We're moving from:
"How do I get someone to open this email?"
To:
"How do I make sure the AI understands this email before the subscriber ever sees it?"
